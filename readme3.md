# **Stress, Strain and Deformation of Solids: Complete Notes**

## **1. Rigid Bodies vs. Deformable Solids**

### **Rigid Body (рдХрдбрд╝рд╛ рдкрд┐рдВрдб)**
*   **Kya Hai?** Yeh ek aisa perfect (рдЖрджрд░реНрд╢) body hai jiska **shape aur size kabhi nahi badalta**, chahe kitna bhi force lagao. Hume pata hai real life mein aisa kuch nahi hota, par calculations aasan karne ke liye hum maan lete hain.
*   **Example:** Jaise hi koi block force lagane par apna shape change na kare, woh rigid body hai.

### **Deformable Solid (рд╡рд┐рдХреГрдд рд╣реЛрдиреЗ рд╡рд╛рд▓рд╛ рдареЛрд╕)**
*   **Kya Hai?** Yeh woh body hoti hai jiska **shape aur size force lagane par badal jata hai**. Real life ke saare solids, jaise steel, rubber, plastic, yeh sab deformable hote hain.
*   **Example:** Rubber band khinchne par lambi ho jati hai. Steel ka rod bhi thoda sa bend ho jata hai.

## **2. Tension, Compression & Shear Stresses (рддрдирд╛рд╡, рд╕рдВрдкреАрдбрд╝рди рдФрд░ рдХрддрд░рдиреА рдкреНрд░рддрд┐рдмрд▓)**

**Stress (рдкреНрд░рддрд┐рдмрд▓)** = Force (рдмрд▓) / Area (рдХреНрд╖реЗрддреНрд░рдлрд▓)
Its SI unit is **Pascal (Pa)** or **N/m┬▓**.

### **(a) Tensile Stress (рддрдиреНрдп рдкреНрд░рддрд┐рдмрд▓ - рдЦрд┐рдЪрд╛рд╡)**
*   **Kya Hai?** Jab kisi object ko kheechkar uski length badhaye.
*   **Formula:**
    `╧Г_t = P / A`
    *   `╧Г_t` = Tensile Stress
    *   `P` = Pulling (Tensile) Force
    *   `A` = Cross-sectional Area

### **(b) Compressive Stress (рд╕рдВрдкреАрдбрди рдкреНрд░рддрд┐рдмрд▓ - рджрдмрд╛рд╡)**
*   **Kya Hai?** Jab kisi object ko dabakar uski length ghataaye.
*   **Formula:**
    `╧Г_c = P / A`
    *   `╧Г_c` = Compressive Stress
    *   `P` = Pushing (Compressive) Force
    *   `A` = Cross-sectional Area

### **(c) Shear Stress (рдХрд░реНрддрди рдкреНрд░рддрд┐рдмрд▓ - рдХрдЯрд╛рд╡)**
*   **Kya Hai?** Jab force aisa lagaye ki object ki ek layer dusri layer ke upar fisalne lage.
*   **Formula:**
    `╧Д = P / A`
    *   `╧Д` = Shear Stress
    *   `P` = Shear Force
    *   `A` = Area resisting shear

**ЁЯУМ Example:** Scissors se paper kaatna, ya fir ek packet chips kholna.

## **3. HookeтАЩs Law & Elastic Constants (рд╣реБрдХ рдХрд╛ рдирд┐рдпрдо рдФрд░ рдкреНрд░рддреНрдпрд╛рд╕реНрдерддрд╛ рдирд┐рдпрддрд╛рдВрдХ)**

### **HookeтАЩs Law (рд╣реБрдХ рдХрд╛ рдирд┐рдпрдо)**
*   **Kya Kehta Hai?** "Elastic limit tak, stress, strain ke **directly proportional (рд╕реАрдзреЗ рд╕рдорд╛рдиреБрдкрд╛рддрд┐рдХ)** hota hai."
*   **Formula:** `Stress тИЭ Strain` OR `E = Stress / Strain`
    *   `E` is Young's Modulus.

### **Elastic Constants (рдкреНрд░рддреНрдпрд╛рд╕реНрдерддрд╛ рдирд┐рдпрддрд╛рдВрдХ)**
Yeh 4 constants hote hain jo material ki stiffness (рдХрдбрд╝рд╛рдкрди) batate hain.

1.  **YoungтАЩs Modulus (E):** `E = Tensile Stress / Tensile Strain`
    *   *Longitudinal (рд▓рдВрдмрд╡рдд) stiffness measure karta hai.*

2.  **Shear Modulus (G):** `G = Shear Stress / Shear Strain`
    *   *Shape change ki resistance measure karta hai.*

3.  **Bulk Modulus (K):** `K = Direct Stress / Volumetric Strain`
    *   *Volume change ki resistance measure karta hai. (Jaise water mein dabav)*

4.  **PoissonтАЩs Ratio (╬╝ or ╬╜):** `╬╜ = - (Lateral Strain / Longitudinal Strain)`
    *   *Jab aap kheechte ho to length badhti hai aur width ghatti hai, uska ratio.*

### **Relations Between Elastic Constants (рд╕реВрддреНрд░)**
In chaaro constants ka aapas mein relation hota hai. Ye formulas yaad rakhna:
*   `E = 2G(1 + ╬╜)`
*   `E = 3K(1 - 2╬╜)`
*   `E = (9KG) / (3K + G)`

## **4. Plastic Behaviour of Materials (рдкреНрд▓рд╛рд╕реНрдЯрд┐рдХ рд╡реНрдпрд╡рд╣рд╛рд░)**

*   **Elastic Region (рдкреНрд░рддреНрдпрд╛рд╕реНрде рдХреНрд╖реЗрддреНрд░):** Jab tak load elastic limit se kam hai, load hatate hi body apna original shape wapas le aati hai. (Jaise spring)
*   **Plastic Region (рдкреНрд▓рд╛рд╕реНрдЯрд┐рдХ рдХреНрд╖реЗрддреНрд░):** Jab load elastic limit se zyada ho jata hai, to body **permanently deform** ho jati hai. Load hatane par bhi shape wapas nahi aati.
*   **Ductile vs Brittle:**
    *   **Ductile Material (рдЖрдШрд╛рддрд╡рд░реНрдзрдиреАрдп):** Ye bahut deform hoke bhi nahi tootega (e.g., Copper, Gold, Steel).
    *   **Brittle Material (рднрдВрдЧреБрд░):** Ye bina zyada deform hue directly toot jayega (e.g., Glass, Cast Iron).

## **5. Deformation of Simple and Compound Bars (рд╡рд┐рдХреГрддрд┐)**

### **(a) Simple Bar (рд╕рд╛рдзрд╛рд░рдг рдЫреЬ)**
Simple bar ki deformation (length mein change) nikalne ka formula:
`╬ФL = (P * L) / (A * E)`
*   `╬ФL` = Change in Length
*   `P` = Axial Load (Tensile or Compressive)
*   `L` = Original Length
*   `A` = Cross-sectional Area
*   `E` = YoungтАЩs Modulus

### **(b) Compound Bars (рдорд┐рд╢реНрд░рд┐рдд рдЫреЬ)**
*   **Series Connection (рд╢реНрд░реГрдВрдЦрд▓рд╛ рдореЗрдВ):** Load sab par same hota hai, total deformation alag-alag bars ki deformation ka sum hota hai.
    `Total ╬ФL = ╬ФL1 + ╬ФL2 + ... = (PL1)/(A1E1) + (PL2)/(A2E2) + ...`

*   **Parallel Connection (рд╕рдорд╛рдирд╛рдВрддрд░ рдореЗрдВ):** Deformation sab bars ki same hoti hai, load alag-alag bars mein bat jata hai.
    `P = P1 + P2` and `╬ФL1 = ╬ФL2`
    Isme load distribution ke liye: `P1/(A1E1) = P2/(A2E2)`

## **6. Thermal Stresses (рддрд╛рдкреАрдп рдкреНрд░рддрд┐рдмрд▓)**

Jab kisi material ko garm kiya jata hai, toh woh failna (expand) chahta hai. Agar use failne nahi diya jata (jaise kisi rod ke dono ends fixed hon), toh uske andar stress paida hota hai. Use kehte hain **Thermal Stress**.

**Formula:**
`╧Г_thermal = E * ╬▒ * ╬ФT`
*   `╧Г_thermal` = Thermal Stress
*   `E` = YoungтАЩs Modulus
*   `╬▒` = Coefficient of thermal expansion (/┬░C)
*   `╬ФT` = Temperature change (┬░C)

**ЁЯУМ Numerical:**
**Q.** A steel rod (E = 200 GPa, ╬▒ = 12 ├Ч 10тБ╗тБ╢ /┬░C) is held between two rigid walls. If its temperature is increased by 50┬░C, calculate the thermal stress developed.
**Sol.:**
`╧Г_thermal = E * ╬▒ * ╬ФT`
`= (200 ├Ч 10тБ╣) ├Ч (12 ├Ч 10тБ╗тБ╢) ├Ч (50)`
`= 120,000,000 Pa` or **120 MPa** (Compressive)

## **7. Volumetric Strains (рдЖрдпрддрдирд┐рдХ рд╡рд┐рдХреГрддрд┐)**

Volumetric Strain (╬╡_v) woh hoti hai jab poore object ka volume change hota hai.
`╬╡_v = Change in Volume / Original Volume = ╬ФV / V`

**Formula for a cube with 3 stresses:**
`╬╡_v = (╧Гx + ╧Гy + ╧Гz)/E * (1 - 2╬╜)`

**Agar sab taraf same pressure 'p' lag raha ho (Hydrostatic Pressure):**
`╬╡_v = (3p / E) * (1 - 2╬╜)`

## **8. Stresses on Inclined Planes (рдЭреБрдХреЗ рд╣реБрдП рддрд▓ рдкрд░ рдкреНрд░рддрд┐рдмрд▓)**

Kisi bhi inclined plane (tircha/tedha plane) par do prakar ke stresses hote hain:
1.  **Normal Stress (╧Г_╬╕):** Jo plane ke perpendicular (рд▓рдВрдмрд╡рдд) lagta hai.
2.  **Shear Stress (╧Д_╬╕):** Jo plane ke parallel (рд╕рдорд╛рдирд╛рдВрддрд░) lagta hai.

**Formulas (if only ╧Гx is applied):**
*   Normal Stress, `╧Г_╬╕ = ╧Гx * cos┬▓╬╕`
*   Shear Stress, `╧Д_╬╕ = (╧Гx / 2) * sin2╬╕`

**Yaha **╬╕** vertical axis se inclined plane ka angle hai.**

**Important Point:** **╬╕ = 45┬░** par **Shear Stress maximum** hota hai. `╧Д_max = ╧Гx / 2`
Isliye ductile materials (jaise steel) 45┬░ ke angle par shear fail hote hain.

## **9. Principal Stresses & Principal Planes (рдореБрдЦреНрдп рдкреНрд░рддрд┐рдмрд▓ рдФрд░ рдореБрдЦреНрдп рддрд▓)**

*   **Principal Planes (рдореБрдЦреНрдп рддрд▓):** Yeh woh special inclined planes hote hain jinka orientation (рдХреЛрдг) aisa hota hai ki **un par shear stress bilkul zero (рд╢реВрдиреНрдп)** hota hai.
*   **Principal Stresses (рдореБрдЦреНрдп рдкреНрд░рддрд┐рдмрд▓):** Principal planes par jo normal stress lagta hai use kehte hain Principal Stress. Yeh **maximum aur minimum possible normal stresses** hote hain pure body par.

**Formulas (for general 2D stress ╧Гx, ╧Гy, ╧Дxy):**
*   **Orientation of Principal Planes:**
    `tan(2╬╕_p) = (2 * ╧Дxy) / (╧Гx - ╧Гy)`
    Is equation se `╬╕_p` nikaloge.

*   **Magnitude of Principal Stresses (╧Г1 & ╧Г2):**
    `╧Г1, ╧Г2 = ( (╧Гx + ╧Гy) / 2 ) ┬▒ тИЪ( [ (╧Гx - ╧Гy) / 2 ]┬▓ + ╧Дxy┬▓ )`
    *   `╧Г1` is always **Maximum** Principal Stress.
    *   `╧Г2` is always **Minimum** Principal Stress.

## **10. MohrтАЩs Circle of Stress (рдореЛрд╣рд░ рдХрд╛ рд╡реГрддреНрдд)**

Mohr's Circle ek graphical method hai jisse aap kisi bhi angle ╬╕ par stress, principal stresses, max shear stress, etc. aaram se find kar sakte ho.

**Steps to Draw Mohr's Circle:**
1.  **Coordinates Banayo:** X-axis = Normal Stress (╧Г), Y-axis = Shear Stress (╧Д).
2.  **Plot Points:** Point C1 (`╧Гx`, `╧Дxy`) and Point C2 (`╧Гy`, `-╧Дxy`).
3.  **Center (C):** Dono points ko milaye, center X-axis par hoga.
    `C = ( (╧Гx + ╧Гy)/2 , 0 )`
4.  **Radius (R):** `R = тИЪ( [ (╧Гx - ╧Гy)/2 ]┬▓ + ╧Дxy┬▓ )`
5.  **Circle Banayo:** Center `C` aur radius `R` leke circle draw karo.

**Results from the Circle:**
*   **Principal Stresses:** Circle jaha X-axis ko katata hai wahi ╧Г1 aur ╧Г2 hote hain.
    `╧Г1 = C + R`, `╧Г2 = C - R`
*   **Maximum Shear Stress:** Circle ka topmost point, `╧Д_max = R`

**ЁЯУМ Example:**
**Q.** For a point, ╧Гx = 80 MPa, ╧Гy = 20 MPa, ╧Дxy = 30 MPa. Find ╧Г1, ╧Г2, ╧Д_max using Mohr's Circle.
**Sol.:**
1.  Center, `C = (80 + 20)/2 = 50 MPa`
2.  Radius, `R = тИЪ( [(80-20)/2]┬▓ + (30)┬▓ ) = тИЪ( (30)┬▓ + (30)┬▓ ) = тИЪ1800 тЙИ 42.43 MPa`
3.  **╧Г1 = C + R = 50 + 42.43 = 92.43 MPa**
4.  **╧Г2 = C - R = 50 - 42.43 = 7.57 MPa**
5.  **╧Д_max = R = 42.43 MPa**
